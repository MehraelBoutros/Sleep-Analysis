#  sleep-quality-academic-performance-analysis
##  Project Overview

This project was developed as part of a university research study to investigate how sleep quality, sleep deprivation, stress levels, and study habits influence academic performance among university students.

The project combines **statistical analysis**, **exploratory data analysis (EDA)**, **hypothesis testing**, and **machine learning** to extract meaningful insights from survey data collected from university students.

---

##  Research Objectives

- Analyze the relationship between sleep quality and academic performance.
- Investigate the impact of sleep duration on students' stress levels.
- Evaluate whether sleep disorders affect academic performance.
- Identify the most common symptoms associated with insufficient sleep.
- Examine the relationship between study timing and academic performance.
- Analyze gender differences in stress levels.
- Build a machine learning model to predict academic performance.

---

##  Dataset

The dataset was collected through an online questionnaire distributed to university students.

It contains information about:

- Gender
- Sleep Duration
- Sleep Quality
- Stress Level
- Study Timing
- Academic Performance
- Sleep Disorders
- Sleep Deprivation Symptoms
- Students' Opinions about Stress and Sleep Improvement

##  Machine Learning

A **Random Forest Classifier** was developed to predict students' academic performance based on:

- Sleep Duration
- Sleep Quality
- Stress Level
- Gender
- Study Timing
- Sleep Disorders

### Model Evaluation

- Train/Test Split
- Classification Report
- Confusion Matrix
- Accuracy Score

Although the predictive accuracy was relatively low, the model demonstrated the importance of evaluating feature relevance and highlighted that academic performance depends on additional factors beyond sleep and stress.

---

##  Key Findings

- No statistically significant relationship was found between sleep quality and academic performance.
- Sleep duration showed no significant effect on stress levels.
- Study timing did not significantly influence academic performance.
- Sleep disorders showed no significant effect on students' grades.
- Female students reported significantly higher stress levels than male students.
- Headaches, mood changes, and lack of concentration were the most frequently reported symptoms of insufficient sleep.
- Academic performance appears to be influenced by additional academic, psychological, and environmental factors beyond the variables included in this study.
